Acoustic Classroom (Master Thesis)

The thesis explored the application of VR to enhance acoustic awareness among school teachers, focusing on the simulation of classroom acoustics within an interactive virtual environment (VE). Furthermore, a real-time hearing loss simulator was developed in C++, to experience the decreased speech intelligibility of a hearing imparied individual. The application was developed through an iterative provess and the effectiveness of the final prototype was evaluated through a series of tests conducted with school teachers.

  • Implementing ray-based geometrical acoustic using Steam Audio

MuCIcal Adventures (Bachelors Project)

This project focused on developing and implementing a VR based Musical Training Program to improve music perception for cochlear implant users. A prototype VR training program was created with three levels targeting sound localization, melody recognition, and musical instrument identification. The initial prototype was tested by three CI users using a user-centered design approach. Further testing at the CoolHear Workshop and Soundcluster Workshop led to additional adjustments. The final version underwent a week-long diary study with one participant, including five sessions. Data was collected through post-training questionnaires, tracking time and errors, and a semi-structured interview. Results showed the participant felt confident in melody recognition but found sound localization challenging.

Implementation skills:

  • Developing a VR application in Unity
  • Writing C# scripts for Unity
  • Spatialization of audio using Steam Audio
The project was developed with co-student Signe Kromann, and was presented as a demo at SMC Conference 2022.

Real-time hearing loss simulator for FMOD

Real-time simulation of a hearing loss based on "Investigation of a Real-Time Hearing Loss Simulation for use in an Audio Production" by Mourgela et al.. Genrated as a plugin for FMOD to be used in game engines. The plugin includes high frequency attenuation, gammatone seperation, spectral smearing, rapid loudness growth and temporal disruption, all based on how a hearing impaired individual perceives sound.

Implementation skills:

  • Real-time signal processing in C++

GameHear: gamification of Conditioned Play Audiometry

Conditioned Play Audiometry is a modification of pure tone audiometry for 3-5-year-old children, turning the hearing test into a playful game. Currently, analog methods are used, such as putting a toy in a box, when a tone is perceived. It is hard to keep the attention of children, who are used to playing with computers, smartphones, etc., at home. At the Copenhagen Hearing and Balance Centre, they report up to one in every third child goes home with little to no results, as the child does not engage in the test. This paper explores gamifying methods for CPA to address low patient cooperation. The current setup at CHBC was transformed into a 3D play audiometer controlled by the child‚Äôs movements using the Azure Kinect. Audiologists evaluated the prototypes using the think-aloud method and non-participatory observations. .

Implementation skills:

  • Implementation of computer vision using Azure Kinect
  • Development of games in Unity
  • Implementation of a digital audiometer in Faust
